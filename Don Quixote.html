Don Quixote

"Don Quixote" by Miguel de Cervantes is a groundbreaking work that intertwines humor, tragedy, and profound philosophy. The novel follows the adventures of Don Quixote, an aging nobleman whose obsession with chivalric ideals leads him to embark on a series of delusional quests, believing himself to be a knight-errant. Alongside him is his loyal squire, Sancho Panza, who offers a stark contrast with his grounded, pragmatic view of the world. Through its satirical narrative, Don Quixote critiques societal norms, mocks the romanticized notions of knighthood, and explores the complexities of reality and illusion. Cervantes' masterpiece is not only a comedic adventure but also a deeply introspective examination of human nature, identity, and the tension between dreams and reality. The novel's influence on literature is immeasurable, marking the transition from medieval narratives to the modern novel.
